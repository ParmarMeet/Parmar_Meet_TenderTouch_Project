Your Burning Questions

What are hemorrhoids?

Hemorrhoids are swollen veins in the lower rectum and anus, similar to varicose veins. They can be caused by increased pressure in the lower rectum, often due to factors like straining during bowel movements, sitting for long periods, or pregnancy.

What are the common symptoms of hemorrhoids?

Common symptoms include pain, itching, swelling, and bleeding during bowel movements. You may also notice a lump near your anus, which can be sensitive or painful.

How can I prevent hemorrhoids?

To prevent hemorrhoids, maintain a healthy diet high in fiber, drink plenty of water, and exercise regularly. Avoid straining during bowel movements and take breaks if sitting for long periods.

What are some treatment options for hemorrhoids?

Treatment options can include over-the-counter creams and ointments, warm baths, and lifestyle changes. In severe cases, medical procedures such as rubber band ligation or surgery may be necessary.

When should I see a doctor?

If you experience significant pain, bleeding that doesn’t stop, or if your symptoms persist despite home treatment, it’s important to see a healthcare provider for a proper diagnosis and treatment plan.
